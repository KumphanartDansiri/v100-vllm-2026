# Qwen3.5-122B-A10B

Qwen3.5-122B-A10B is the **flagship: 122B-total / 10B-active MoE**, and the clearest payoff of an
8×V100-32GB box — a frontier-class model serving at a comfortable rate on hardware the modern stacks
call e-waste. Two checkpoints are measured at TP8: our **FP8 (resident)** plugin path and stock
**GPTQ-Int4** (vLLM's own kernels).

- **What fits / best config:** **TP8** (the model needs all 8 cards either way), cudagraph,
  `--dtype float16`, `--skip-mm-profiling`, `max-model-len=32768`. FP8 adds the plugin with grouped
  coalesced MoE GEMV on all 8 ranks; Int4 is stock vLLM.
- **Runs on both engines** (`Qwen3MoeForCausalLM`). FP8 is our sm_70 kernels (engine-invariant by
  construction); Int4 is stock.

## How to read it
- **Both precisions are comfortably usable** — even at 8 concurrent users per-user stays above 30
  tok/s (FP8 30.7 / Int4 36.8 on 0.21; 34.9 / 41.2 on 0.19), and single-stream is ~57–69 tok/s. A
  122B model, interactive, on 7-year-old GPUs.
- **Int4 edges FP8 on raw decode** (C1 58.1 vs 56.6 on 0.21; 69.3 vs 67.3 on 0.19) and on 8-user
  aggregate (294 vs 246 on 0.21) — fewer weight bytes (4-bit < 8-bit) means more bandwidth headroom.
  FP8 keeps higher numerical fidelity and is **our** path; Int4 is the stock baseline it's measured
  against. Both are legitimate ways to serve this model on V100.
- **0.19 is faster than 0.21** for both checkpoints (FP8 67 vs 57 C1). Cold TTFT ~18–20 s either way.

## Caveats
- FP8 is **Stable** (MoE@TP8, not bit-identical run-to-run — expert/all-reduce ordering); Int4 is
  **Exact** on 0.21.
- **TP8 only** — this model does not fit at lower TP on 32 GB cards.
- FP8 is our resident sm_70 plugin path; Int4 is stock vLLM (gptq kernels).
