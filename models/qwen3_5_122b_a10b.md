# Qwen3.5-122B-A10B (MoE — FP8 + GPTQ-Int4) — V100 model-family page

> **Status: Final** — numbers frozen from `data/benchmark_matrix.csv` (perf_v2 rows, tag `fp8-v100-2026-matrix`); digest tables auto-render and the exhaustive raw SSOT table is at the bottom. Refresh procedure: [../docs/FINAL_RERUN.md](../docs/FINAL_RERUN.md).

The **flagship: 122B-total / 10B-active MoE**, and the clearest payoff of an 8×V100-32GB box — a
frontier-class model serving interactively on hardware the modern stacks call e-waste.

## Family / checkpoints
- `Qwen/Qwen3.5-122B-A10B-FP8` — our FP8 (resident) plugin path.
- `Qwen/Qwen3.5-122B-A10B-GPTQ-Int4` — stock vLLM GPTQ-Int4 (the comparison baseline).
- **Compatibility:** runs on vLLM **0.19 and 0.21 stock** (no transformers-5 upgrade); **TP8 only**;
  the MoE patch doesn't apply (no FP16 path). (Chapter 6 matrix.)

## Fit / compatibility
- **TP8 only** — the model needs all 8 cards either way; it does **not** fit at lower TP on 32 GB cards.
- **FP16 is omitted** — ~244 GB, not a practical serving baseline on 8×V100; FP8 and Int4 are the real
  choices.
- cudagraph, `--dtype float16`, `--skip-mm-profiling`, `max-model-len=32768`. FP8 adds the plugin with
  grouped coalesced MoE GEMV on all 8 ranks; Int4 is stock vLLM.
- **Best engine:** 0.19 for decode throughput (faster at every point); 0.21 also works.

## First-token latency (TTFT)
*Single-stream time to first token: **cold** (a fresh, cache-cold full prefill — worst case) vs **prefix-cache-hit** (repeated / shared context — best case). Decode is the headline; this is the latency side.*

<!-- render:ttft:qwen3_5_122b_a10b -->
| Choice | Engine | Cold First Token | FA-on Cold | Prefix-cache Hit |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| FP8 TP8 | 0.19 | 82.020 s | — | 4.812 s |
|  | 0.21 | 88.430 s | 87.38 s | 5.346 s |
| GPTQ-Int4 TP8 | 0.19 | 25.933 s | — | 1.240 s |
|  | 0.21 | 25.650 s | — | 1.233 s |

All TTFT is single-stream, chunked-prefill **on** (the project-standard serve — disabling chunked prefill is a known V100 crash-causer). **Cold first-token** = a fresh, cache-cold request prefilling the full ~22.6k-token prompt (worst case); **Prefix-cache-hit** = the same prompt with its prefix already cached — repeated or shared context (best case). Cold TTFT is prefill-bound, and the Qwen **block-FP8** checkpoints carry a large prefill penalty on V100 (an unoptimized FP8-prefill path, worst on the MoE models) — a latency-side current-state limit, not where FP8's *decode* win lives; compressed-tensors FP8 (Gemma/GLM) and FP16/Int4 prefill cheaper.
<!-- endrender -->

They trade the lead through mid-concurrency; **Int4 pulls clear only at the C8 aggregate** (294 vs 246
on 0.21) — fewer weight bytes (4-bit < 8-bit) → more bandwidth headroom. Even at 8 users, per-user stays
above 30 tok/s.

## FP8 vs Int4 — which to serve
| Choice | Best for | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|
| **FP8** (our plugin) | higher fidelity (e4m3 > int4); the broad-fleet path; MTP runs on it | a few % slower than Int4 at the C8 aggregate; **~3× slower cold first-token** (88 s vs 26 s — block-FP8 prefill is unoptimized; see the TTFT table) |
| **GPTQ-Int4** (stock) | max C8 aggregate throughput; **much faster cold prefill** | needs a GPTQ checkpoint; lower bit depth; MTP crashes on it |

## Caveats
- FP8 is **Stable** (MoE@TP8, not bit-identical run-to-run); Int4 is **Exact** on 0.21.
- **TP8 only**; `max-model-len=32768`.
